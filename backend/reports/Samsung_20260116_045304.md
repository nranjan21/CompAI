## Company Overview
Samsung Group, founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul, began as a trading company and has since diversified into various sectors, including food processing, textiles, and insurance. The company made a significant shift in the late 1960s by entering the electronics industry, which has since become a major growth driver. Following the founder's death in 1987, Samsung was restructured into five business groups, with Samsung Electronics emerging as the most prominent subsidiary. Headquartered in Samsung Town, Seoul, South Korea, the company employs a vast workforce across its various divisions.

Key products include:
- **Galaxy Smartphones**
- **Televisions**
- **Home Appliances**
- **Semiconductors**
- **Wearables**

Historically, Samsung Electronics has been a global leader in the electronics sector, heavily investing in research and development, particularly during the 1980s and 1990s. The company became the world's largest producer of memory chips by 1992 and has since contributed to iconic construction projects like the Burj Khalifa and Petronas Towers.
